The graph shows Google Search Trends for terms related to "Deepfake" and "AudioSpooing".

In 2023, deepfakes significantly contributed to identity theft incidents, with the number of AI-generated deepfakes increasing tenfold compared to 2022. Deepfakes, alongside other AI-powered fraud techniques, became one of the top five tools used for fraud, including identity theft. Specifically, deepfakes were involved in many identity fraud cases, accounting for a significant portion of the $43 billion lost to identity fraud in the US. The technology has made it easier for criminals to create realistic forgeries, particularly targeting ID cards, which were the most exploited document for identity fraud, representing nearly 73% of fraudulent activities involving identity documents

  • 10x increase globally in deepfake fraud cases from 2022 to early 2023.
  • Crypto sector accounted for 88% of all deepfake cases detected in 2023, fintech 8%.
  • Regional differences in deepfake growth rates from 2022 to 2023: North America: 1,740%; APAC: 1,530%; Europe: 780%; MEA: 450%; Latin America: 410%.
  • Deepfake identity fraud doubled from 2022 to just Q1 2023 in the research study.
  • 90% of consumers raised concerns about deepfake attacks.
  • 70% percent of people could not confidently differentiate between a real voice and a cloned one.
  • 10% of those surveyed had received a message from an AI voice clone, and 77% of these people lost money as a consequence.
  • In 2023, the cost of purchasing ready-made deepfakes varied between $300 to $20,000 per minute, depending on complexity, quality, and the fame of the impersonated person.
  • Video call verification methods are highly vulnerable to deepfake attacks that spoof identity documents and faces (German Federal Office for Information Security warning).
  • Cybersecurity experts have urged EU policymakers to reconsider the vulnerability disclosure requirements under the proposed Cyber Resilience Act.
